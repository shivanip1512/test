Unit of Measure

The units of measure for the data being read can be set to the following values:

  • kW – Unit used to measure power, where one kW is equal to one thousand watts.

  • kWh – Unit used to measure power, where one kWh is equal to one thousand watt hours.

  • kVA – Unit used to measure apparent power, where one KVa is equal to one thousand volt-amperes.

  • kVAr – Unit used to measure reactive power (kilovolt-ampere reactive).

  • kVAh – Unit used to measure apparent power (klilovolt-ampere hours).

  • kVArh – Unit used to measure reactive power (klilovolt-ampere reactive hours).

  • kVolts – Unit used to measure electric potential energy, where one kV is equal to one thousand volts.

  • kQ – Unit used to measure Reactive power compensation factor.

  • Amps – Unit used to measure electrical current.

  • Counts – Unit used to measure the number of occurrences.

  • Degrees – Unit used to measure an angle.

  • Dollars – Unit used to measure currency.

  • Dollar Char – Symbol of the currency measurement.

  • Feet – Unit used to measure length.

  • Gallons – Unit used to measure the volume of liquids.

  • Gal/PM – Unit used to measure the volumetric flow of liquids.

  • GAS-CFT – Unit used to measure the volume of a gas in cubic feet.

  • Hours – Unit used to measure time.

  • Level – Unit used measure the relative position on a scale.

  • Minutes – Unit used to measure time.

  • MW – Unit used to measure power, where one MW is equal to one million watts.

  • MWh – Unit used to measure power, where one MWh is equal to one million watt hours.

  • MVA – Unit used to measure apparent power, where one KVa is equal to one million volt-amperes.

  • MVAr – Unit used to measure reactive power (megavolt-ampere reactive).

  • MVAh – Unit used to measure apparent power (megavolt-ampere hours).

  • MVArh – Unit used to measure reactive power (megavolt-ampere reactive hours).

  • Ops – Unit used to measure the number of operations.

  • PF – Unit used to measure power factor, which is the ratio of the real power to the apparent power.

  • Percent – Unit used to measure the ratio of a whole, which is typically a fraction of 100.

  • Percent Char – Symbol of the percent measurement.

  • PSI – Unit used to measure pressure, where one PSI is one pound per square inch.

  • Seconds – Unit used to measure time.

  • Temp-F – Unit used to measure temperature in degrees Fahrenheit.

  • Temp-C – Unit used to measure temperature in degrees Celsius.

  • Vars – Unit used to measure reactive power (volt-ampere reactive).

  • Volts – Unit used to measure electric potential energy.

  • VoltAmps – Unit used to measure apparent power, which is equal to the product of voltage and current.

  • VA – Unit used to measure apparent power, which is equal to the product of voltage and current.

  • Water-CFT – Unit used to measure the volume of water in cubic feet.

  • Watts – Unit used to measure power, where one watt is equal to one joule per second.

  • Hertz – Unit used to measure frequency, where one hertz has a periodic interval of one second.

  • Volts from V2H – Unit used to measure volts from a volt-squared-hour (V2H) meter.

  • Amps from A2H – Unit used to measure amps from an ampere-squared-hour (A2H)  meter.

  • LTC Tap Position – Unit used to measure the tap position on a Load Tap Changer.

  • Miles – Unit used to measure length.

  • Milliseconds – Unit used to measure time.

  • Parts Per Million – Unit used to measure relative proportions in measured quantities.

  • Miles Per Hour – Unit used to measure velocity.

  • Inches – Unit used to measure length.

  • Kilometers Per Hour – Unit used to measure velocity.

  • Millibars – Unit used to measure pressure.

  • Kilometers Per Hour – Unit used to measure velocity.

  • Meter Per Second – Unit used to measure velocity.

  • kVolts – Unit used to measure electric potential energy, where one kV is equal to one thousand volts.

  • Undefined – Unit of measure that is not defined.
